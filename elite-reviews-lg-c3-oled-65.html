What Could Be Better ü§î

1. Not Bright Enough for Very Bright Rooms

OLED has incredible contrast, but peak brightness is only about 800 nits:

  • Can wash out in rooms with lots of sunlight
  • Mini-LED/QLED TVs get brighter (1000-2000 nits)
  • Best in controlled lighting or darker rooms

If you have a very sunny living room, consider the Samsung QN90C (Mini-LED) instead.

2. Risk of Burn-In (Though Rare)

OLED panels can suffer from burn-in if you:

  • Leave static images on screen for hours daily (news tickers, game HUDs)
  • Use as a PC monitor with taskbars

LG has burn-in prevention features, and it's rare with normal use. But it's still a risk to be aware of.

3. Built-In Speakers Are Just OK

The speakers aren't bad, but they're not great either:

  • Thin sound with weak bass
  • Can get a bit harsh at high volume

You'll want a soundbar or home theater system for the best experience. We recommend the Sonos Beam or Arc.

4. Only 60Hz for 1080p/1440p

If you play older consoles or use a PC at 1080p/1440p, you're limited to 60Hz. Only native 4K gets 120Hz. Not a huge deal, but worth noting.
